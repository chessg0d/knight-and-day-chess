The challenge

Not every kid comes to chess the same way.

Some have never played and think it looks boring or hard.
Others already love it and want to get genuinely good.

One message for both would fail. So the campaign speaks to two audiences.

The academy

Two programs, one academy

🔥 Spark

For brand new players. Playful, pressure free, all about falling in love with the game.

♟️ Sharpen

For keen players who want to improve: tactics, openings, endgames, tournament prep.

Audience

Who I'm writing for

  • Parents, the decision makers. They care about focus, confidence, and a welcoming first experience.
  • Kids & teens, motivated by progress and challenge; they influence the family's choice.
Platforms

The right voice in the right place

  • Facebook → parents · longer, trust building posts
  • Instagram → kids + parents · puzzles, tips, Reels
  • Reddit → chess community + locals · value first, no hard sell
  • TikTok → the campaign video

One goal: book a free trial on the website.

Website homepage

The conversion point

Green brand palette · clear headline · two programs side by side · scannable copy · repeated free trial CTA · accessible.

Social media campaign

Same brand, different voice

Warm & reassuring for parents · fast & fun for kids · unified by color, "Spark/Sharpen" language, and hashtags.

Persuasive strategy

Ethos · Pathos · Logos

  • Ethos, honest, defensible benefits (focus, planning), no overpromising.
  • Pathos, real moments: a kid's first checkmate, a relieved parent.
  • Logos, free to try, no experience needed, a fit for every child.
Sustainability

Built to last past launch

  • Swap placeholder testimonials for real student stories.
  • Simple weekly rhythm: a puzzle, a tip, a spotlight, an event.
  • Referrals + an email list from free trial sign ups.
